Experience

Seven years inside healthcare operations.

Most of my work is internal tools — the software that call-center agents, clinicians, claims adjusters, and billing teams stare at for eight hours a day. It’s where small friction compounds into millions of dollars and thousands of bad appointments.

LifeStance Health Lead Product Designer · Remote
Jun 2024 – present

Design across the 33-state footprint

  • Enabled the national rollout of an internal booking tool by removing operational bottlenecks and aligning cross-functional priorities. $500K annual savings; $15–25M in unlocked revenue capacity.
  • Modeled a 9–11× ROI from the current design team, plus a scoped 40–100× upside through targeted hires — used by the new CTO for resource planning.
  • Led strategy and design for a patient–provider routing system; turned an ambiguous funnel into a measurable framework for conversion, capacity, and intent.
  • Influenced leadership to treat design as a revenue lever, not a cost center.

Hello

Work that’s mine — and does some good.

I’m Cody. I came to product design through illustration and a few years in operations at Postmates and Twitter, and I’ve spent the last seven years designing tools for healthcare.

For a while I let the work be measured by what it handed back to me — the title, the raise, the approval. I chased those, got them, and found they don’t add up to much on their own. What pulls at me now is different: work that’s unmistakably mine, that includes more people than it leaves out, and that treats the person on the other side of the screen with real care.

I’m learning to be more open with it, too — to make room for other designers, to write down what I’ve figured out and give it away, and to let this craft stay a little joyful. A reader’s atlas is one of those for-the-joy-of-it projects, and I keep an hour a week free for any designer who wants to talk.
